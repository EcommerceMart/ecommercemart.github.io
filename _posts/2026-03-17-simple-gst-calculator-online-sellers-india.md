### Understanding the Types of GST: CGST, SGST, and IGST

GST isn't just one big tax. It's broken down into different types depending on where you sell your goods or services. Don't worry, it's simpler than it sounds!

#### **CGST (Central Goods and Services Tax)**

This is the part of GST that goes to the Central Government of India. When you sell something *within* the same state (for example, from Mumbai to Pune), you charge both CGST and SGST.

So, if the total GST rate is 18%, then 9% will be CGST.

#### **SGST (State Goods and Services Tax)**

This is the part of GST that goes to the State Government where the sale happens. Like CGST, you charge SGST when you sell something *within* the same state.

If the total GST rate is 18%, then the other 9% will be SGST. This is why for intra-state sales, you often see GST split into two equal parts on invoices.

#### **IGST (Integrated Goods and Services Tax)**

This is a special type of GST for sales that happen *between different states* (for example, from Mumbai to Delhi). IGST combines both the central and state portions of the tax. So, you only charge IGST, not CGST and SGST separately.

If the total GST rate is 18%, you would simply charge 18% IGST for an inter-state sale. This makes cross-state selling easier for you as an online seller! Our `GST calculator for online sellers India` helps with the combined rate, regardless of type.

### Input Tax Credit (ITC): What it Means for You

Input Tax Credit, or ITC, is a super helpful feature of the GST system. It's like a discount you get on the GST you pay.

Imagine you're an online seller. You buy raw materials or products from a supplier, and you pay GST on those purchases. When you sell your finished product, you collect GST from your customers.

ITC allows you to reduce the GST you have to pay to the government by the amount of GST you already paid on your purchases. It means you only pay the "net" tax. This prevents taxes from piling up at each step of the supply chain, which is great for businesses.

While our calculator helps with `GST rate calculation India` for sales, understanding ITC is vital for your overall tax liability. Always keep proper records of your GST paid on inputs.

#### **Q1: What is the main purpose of GST for online sellers in India?**

A3: You need to identify the HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) code for your product. #### **Q4: What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST?**

#### **Q5: Can I claim back the GST I pay on my purchases?**
A5: Yes, this is called Input Tax Credit (ITC). If you are GST registered, you can generally claim credit for the GST you pay on raw materials, goods, or services used for your business. This reduces your overall GST liability.

A6: Most online sellers are required to file monthly GST returns (GSTR-1 for sales, GSTR-3B for summary). There are also annual returns. It's essential to stay updated on filing deadlines to avoid penalties. Using an `online GST calculation India` tool helps prepare your data.
