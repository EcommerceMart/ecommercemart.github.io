## 10 Common Shopify Myths New Sellers Still Believe (and the Truth)

Starting an online store can feel like stepping into a whole new world. You might hear lots of different ideas about what it takes to succeed. Sometimes, these ideas are not quite right, and they can make you feel worried. Many new sellers have **shopify beginner fears** because of common misinformation.

But don't worry! We are here to clear things up. Is **shopify legit for beginners**? Absolutely, but only if you know the facts. We're going to look at ten big **shopify myths** that often scare new people away. We'll show you why these **shopify scam myths** are wrong and give you the real truth.

Let's dive into the world of "Myth vs. Reality" and boost your confidence!

### Myth 1: Shopify is Too Expensive to Start

**Truth:** Many people think you need a lot of money just to open a store on Shopify. This is one of the biggest **shopify myths** out there. While there are different plans, Shopify offers very affordable options for new businesses.

You can actually start with a basic plan that costs less than a fancy coffee each day. This plan gives you all the tools you need to build a professional store. You don't need to spend thousands of dollars to get going.

Shopify also includes website hosting, security, and customer support in its plans. If you tried to set all of that up yourself, it would cost a lot more. Think of it as renting a really good shop space, but online and much cheaper!

### Myth 2: You Need to Be a Tech Genius to Use Shopify

**Truth:** This is another common **shopify beginner fear**. Some people believe you must know how to code or be an expert with computers. This is simply not true at all. Shopify is built for everyone, even if you've never built a website before.

It uses a simple drag-and-drop system. This means you can just click and move things around to design your store. There are lots of ready-made designs (called themes) that you can pick from. You just add your products and pictures, and you're good to go!

Plus, there are thousands of helpful apps in the Shopify App Store. These apps can add extra features to your store without any tricky coding. Shopify truly makes it easy for anyone to create a beautiful online shop. This makes **shopify legit for beginners** because of its user-friendly design.

### Myth 3: You Need to Have Your Own Products to Sell

**Truth:** This is one of the biggest **shopify dropshipping myths**. Many new sellers think they need to make, store, and ship their own items. However, a popular business model called "dropshipping" changes all of that.

With dropshipping, you sell products online without ever holding any inventory yourself. When a customer buys something from your store, you simply order it from a supplier. The supplier then ships the product directly to your customer.

This means you don't need a warehouse or to worry about packaging items. It's a fantastic way for beginners to start with very little upfront cost. There are even tools to help you find reliable suppliers.

### Myth 4: Shopify is Only for Big Businesses with Huge Budgets

**Truth:** This is a common misconception that scares away many small business owners. People see big brands using Shopify and think it's out of their league. However, Shopify is designed to work for businesses of all sizes, from tiny startups to giant companies.

It's actually perfect for small businesses, artists, crafters, and independent sellers. Shopify offers plans that grow with your business. You can start small and then upgrade as your sales increase. This flexibility is what makes **shopify legit for beginners** and small businesses alike.

Shopify provides the same powerful tools and secure platform to everyone, regardless of size. So, whether you're selling a few handmade items or planning a global brand, Shopify can support you. Don't let the size of other stores make you think you're too small.

### Myth 5: Your Shopify Store Will Automatically Get Sales

**Truth:** This is a myth that can lead to disappointment if you believe it. Simply building a great-looking store on Shopify doesn't guarantee sales. Your store is like a physical shop in a big city; people need to know it exists to visit.

You need to tell people about your store and products. This involves marketing, which is how you get the word out. Things like sharing on social media, using Google search ads, or sending out emails are all part of marketing. You'll need to put in effort to attract customers.

Think of Shopify as your powerful shop building, but you're still in charge of advertising it. Learning some basic marketing strategies will be key to your success. Don't expect sales to just magically appear; you need to work for them!

### Myth 7: You Need a Lot of Money to Start Dropshipping

**Truth:** This is another common **shopify dropshipping myth** that can hold beginners back. While starting any business requires *some* investment, dropshipping on Shopify can be done with a very low budget. You don't need thousands of dollars.

Your main costs will be your Shopify subscription and perhaps some money for initial marketing. Since you don't buy products upfront, your risk is much lower. You only pay for goods after your customer has paid you. This makes it an incredibly appealing option for those with limited funds.

You can start with just the basic Shopify plan and free themes. Then, you can invest more in ads or premium apps as your profits grow. It's a very lean way to begin an eCommerce business.

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

#### Q1: Is Shopify really safe for my customer's payment information?
A1: Yes, Shopify is extremely secure. They are certified Level 1 PCI DSS compliant, which means they meet the highest global standards for payment processing security. Your customer's data is protected with strong encryption. This directly answers "is **shopify safe**?" with a confident yes.

#### Q2: Can I really start a Shopify store with very little money?
A2: Absolutely! You can start with a basic Shopify plan, which is quite affordable. If you choose a dropshipping model, your product costs are only incurred *after* a customer pays you. This significantly reduces your initial investment. It truly makes **shopify legit for beginners** on a budget.

#### Q3: Do I need a business license to sell on Shopify?
A3: This depends on where you live and the type of products you sell. In many places, if you're selling goods regularly, you will need some form of business registration or license. It's always best to check with your local government or a legal expert. Shopify provides the platform, but you are responsible for legal compliance.

#### Q4: How long does it take to set up a Shopify store?
A4: You can have a basic store set up in a day or two! Adding all your products, refining your design, and setting up marketing can take longer. It depends on how much time you dedicate. The platform itself is very quick and easy to navigate, busting many **shopify beginner fears** about setup complexity.

#### Q5: What if I don't know what to sell?
A5: Many beginners start without a clear product idea. You can use market research tools, look at trending products, or consider things you're passionate about. Dropshipping also allows you to test many different products without buying inventory. Don't let "what to sell" become a major one of your **shopify beginner fears**.

#### Q6: Are there hidden fees with Shopify that make it a **shopify scam myth**?
A6: Shopify is transparent about its fees. There's the monthly subscription fee for your chosen plan, and transaction fees if you don't use Shopify Payments. Apps from the App Store might also have their own fees. All these are clearly outlined, so there are no hidden surprises. It's a legitimate business, not a scam.

#### Q7: Can I sell digital products (like e-books or courses) on Shopify?
A7: Yes, Shopify is great for selling digital products too! You can set up your store to deliver digital files instantly after purchase. This is another flexible option for new sellers, expanding beyond physical goods.

#### Q8: Is dropshipping still profitable, or is it too competitive?
A8: While dropshipping has become more popular, it is still very profitable if done correctly. Success comes from finding good products, targeting the right audience, and offering excellent customer service. Don't let **shopify dropshipping myths** about saturation deter you; focus on unique value.

#### Q9: If I decide to stop selling, is it easy to close my Shopify account?
A9: Yes, Shopify makes it straightforward to pause or close your store. You can pause your store for a lower fee if you need a break, or fully close it from your account settings if you decide to stop entirely.
